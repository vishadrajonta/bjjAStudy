Takedowns

Barriers To Takedown

The main barriers to taking your opponent down is what stops you from getting to his legs with your hands. This includes his head, hands and elbows.

There are three ways to get around these barriers. The first is to get your partner's hands on the mat, so that they are occupied with keeping their balance and not defending your takedown.

The second way, to beat your training partner's elbows, is to get outside them.

The third way, to beat your training partner's head, is to get them to raise their posture, thus opening up a path into their legs which you can do with a level-change.
